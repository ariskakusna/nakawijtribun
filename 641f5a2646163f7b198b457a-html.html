Boxing: Mike Tyson shares the reasons he calls David Benavidez the &quot;Mexican Monster&quot;

 Posted on August 24, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  178 words  |  Billy Koelling

As we're hours away from the fight between David Benavidez and Caleb Plant in Las Vegas, both boxers seem eager to get inside the ring after their beef reached a new height during the weigh-in face off.

Both fighters stared each other in the eyes and started trash talking, with a staff member getting in the way trying to avoid any further issues, but unfortunately for him, at that moment Plant placed his hand on Benavidez's neck, with the "Mexican Monster" shoving his rival, with Caleb Plant being forced to take a step back.

Mike Tyson shares why he gave David Benavidez the "Mexican Monster" nickname

Ahead of the fight between David Benavidez and Caleb Plant this Saturday March 25th in Las Vegas, Mike Tyson shared the reason he nicknamed Benavidez as the "Mexican Monster."

"Listen I'd be afraid to fight him too if I was in that division. No one even has a chance against him as it appears to be and I'm talking from facts," Tyson said about Benavidez who's 26-0 in his career so far.
