Deck the Halls (of the Burj Khalifa, That Is): How Dubai Does Christmas with a Glittering Twist

Christmas in Dubai? You might be thinking sunshine, sandy beaches, and sipping cocktails by the pool – not exactly the traditional festive fare. But fear not, merrymakers! Dubai throws a Christmas bash that's as over-the-top and glamorous as its reputation suggests. Forget fireplaces and frosty mornings; here's how Dubai flips the script on the holidays.

Light Up Your Life (Literally)

Dubai isn't known for subtlety, and Christmas is no exception. The city transforms into a wonderland of twinkling lights. Imagine the Burj Khalifa, the world's tallest building, dressed up like a giant Christmas tree – that's the kind of dazzling displays you can expect. Sheikh Zayed Road turns into a shimmering tunnel of festive cheer, and every mall goes all out with decorations that would make Santa himself do a double take.

Pro Tip: Pack your sunglasses – the sheer wattage of Dubai's Christmas lights might rival the desert sun!

Sleigh Bells Ring...But Not From Reindeer

Dubai might not have reindeer, but they've got the next best thing: camels! Yes, you read that right. Christmas parades often feature these majestic desert creatures decked out in festive attire (think Santa hats and tinsel). It's a sight that's equal parts hilarious and strangely heartwarming.

Bonus points: If you manage to snag a selfie with a Christmas camel, you've officially won Christmas.

Embrace the Festive Foodie Frenzy

Dubai's restaurants go all out for Christmas, offering everything from traditional roasts with all the trimmings (complete with cranked-up AC to make it feel suitably Christmassy) to festive brunches that are legendary for their sheer scale and variety.

Looking for something a little different? No problem! Sample a special Christmas menu at one of Dubai's many international restaurants, or indulge in a festive afternoon tea that's as fancy as a Dubai skyscraper.

Remember: Book your table well in advance, especially if you have your heart set on a specific Christmas feast. Competition for these coveted spots can be fierce!

How to Christmas in Dubai: FAQ

Q: How do I dress for a Dubai Christmas?

A: Pack for both summer weather and air-conditioned chills. Think sundresses with cardigans or light jackets for exploring the festive lights, and long sleeves for those fancy Christmas dinners.

Q: Are there Christmas markets in Dubai?

A: Absolutely! Dubai embraces the Christmas market trend with pop-up markets offering everything from unique gifts and festive treats to live entertainment.

Q: Can I experience snow in Dubai at Christmas?

A: Believe it or not, yes! Ski Dubai, the indoor ski resort, offers a winter wonderland experience all year round. You can hit the slopes, have a snowball fight, or even build a snowman (with imported snow, of course).

Q: What about religious celebrations?

A: While Christmas isn't a public holiday in Dubai, there are churches that hold Christmas services. Dubai is a multicultural city, and visitors of all faiths are welcome to celebrate in their own way.

Q: Is Dubai expensive at Christmas?

A: Dubai can be expensive any time of year, but there are ways to enjoy Christmas on a budget. Look for free festive events, explore the Christmas markets, and pack some picnic supplies for a festive lunch at the beach.

So, there you have it! Dubai's Christmas celebrations are a unique blend of tradition and over-the-top glitz. It's a chance to experience the festive spirit in a completely new way, with camels, sunshine, and enough lights to rival the Milky Way. Just remember, pack your camera, your sense of humor, and your appetite – Dubai's Christmas is an unforgettable experience!
