Alex Lopez (born June 12, 1978 – died September 14, 2010, Aged: 32 years old) was a Filipino businessman and model who was married to Diana Zubiri, a popular actress in the Philippines. He died of liver cirrhosis in 2010, leaving behind his wife and son. This article will explore his life, career, marriage, death, and legacy.

Early Life and Career

Alex Lopez was born on June 12, 1978, in Manila, Philippines. He was the son of former Philippine Basketball Association (PBA) player Alex Tan and actress Maritess Revilla. He had two siblings, a brother named Carlo and a sister named Nicole.

Lopez attended De La Salle University, where he graduated with a degree in business administration. He also played basketball for the school’s varsity team, the Green Archers.

After college, Lopez pursued a career in modeling and appeared in various print and TV advertisements. He also ventured into business and became the owner of a restaurant and a clothing line.

Alex Lopez’s Marriage to Diana Zubiri

In 2007, Lopez crossed paths with Diana Zubiri, an emerging luminary in the realm of Philippine showbiz. Their romantic journey unfolded over the course of a year, culminating in matrimony on February 14, 2008, in a ceremony imbued with simplicity and legal significance. Zubiri, born Rosemarie Joy Garcia on April 15, 1985, in Bulacan, Philippines, entered the entertainment scene at the age of 15 when Seiko Films discovered her, following her application for work in Japan. This marked the beginning of her venture into the film industry, where she assumed provocative roles in various Seiko productions.

Zubiri’s notoriety soared in October 2002 when she graced the top of the EDSA-Shaw flyover in Mandaluyong, adorned solely in a two-piece bikini for a photo shoot featured in FHM Philippines. The provocative session stirred controversy, leading then-Mayor Benhur Abalos to file charges against Zubiri and FHM. However, the legal action was eventually retracted following a sincere apology.

Transitioning away from her earlier roles, Zubiri shifted her focus to television in 2005, joining GMA Network as a versatile actress. Her portfolio includes noteworthy performances such as portraying Divina in the drama series “Linlang” and embodying the character Danaya in the fantasy series “Encantadia.” Zubiri also graced the cast of the sketch-comedy show “Bubble Gang” and took on lead roles in drama series like “Babangon Ako’t Dudurugin Kita,” “Gaano Kadalas Ang Minsan?” by Danny Zialcita, and “Nita Negrita.”

In the midst of these professional endeavors, Lopez and Zubiri embraced parenthood, welcoming their son, Joaquin Achilles Garcia, affectionately known as King, into the world on June 14, 2008. The union of Lopez and Zubiri thus weaves a tapestry of shared experiences, blending the glitz of the entertainment industry with the joys and responsibilities of family life.

Who is Diana Zubiri?

Diana Zubiri is a Filipino actress who is best known for her roles in Encantadia, Bubble Gang, and Sahaya. She is currently married to Andy Smith, a Filipino-Australian businessman and model. They have two daughters, Aliyah Rose and Amira Jade, and a son, Joaquin Achilles, who is Zubiri’s child from her previous marriage to Alex Lopez, who died in 2010.

Zubiri and Smith’s love story began with a serendipitous blind date, culminating in a private wedding ceremony in New Manila, Quezon City, on May 10, 2015. Their union blossomed with the arrival of their first daughter, Aliyah Rose, on August 14, 2016, and later, the joyous addition of their second daughter, Amira Jade, born on December 14, 2019. Adding to the family dynamic, Zubiri’s son, Joaquin Achilles, affectionately known as King, entered the world on June 14, 2008, a precious legacy from Zubiri’s late husband, Lopez, who sadly succumbed to liver cirrhosis.

The couple decided to embark on a new chapter, relocating to Australia in October 2021, seeking a serene and uncomplicated life. Zubiri expressed that King thrived in the Australian environment, reveling in the freedom and abundant access to parks. The decision to migrate had been in the cards since 2019, but prior commitments in the Philippines necessitated a delay.

Despite the geographical shift, Zubiri has remained dedicated to her acting career, making periodic trips back to the Philippines. In a heartwarming reunion, she joined her Encantadia co-stars, including Iza Calzado, Sunshine Dizon, and Karylle, for a special performance on It’s Showtime in November 2023. Zubiri’s commitment to her craft extended to her roles in GMA drama series such as Beautiful Justice and Dragon Lady in 2019, showcasing her versatility. Additionally, she took on the role of a host in the lifestyle show Taste Buddies, demonstrating her enduring passion for the entertainment industry.

Family Details

Alex Lopez was the son of Alex Tan and Maritess Revilla, both of whom were celebrities in their own right. His father was a former professional basketball player who played for several teams in the Philippine Basketball Association (PBA), such as the Toyota Super Corollas, the Great Taste Coffee Makers, and the Manila Beer Brewmasters. 

He was also a member of the Philippine national team that competed in the 1978 FIBA World Championship and the 1982 Asian Games His mother was an actress who starred in several movies and TV shows in the 1970s and 1980s, such as Ang Pinakamagandang Hayop sa Balat ng Lupa, Ang Alamat ni Julian Makabayan, and Lovingly Yours, Helen. She was also the daughter of the late actor and comedian Amado Cortez.

Alex Lopez had two siblings, a brother named Carlo and a sister named Nicole. Carlo is also a businessman and model who owns a clothing line called Carlo Lopez Clothing. He is married to actress Sunshine Dizon, who is known for her roles in Encantadia, Bakekang, and Ika-6 na Utos. They have two children, a daughter named Doreen and a son named Anton.

What Happened to Alex Lopez?

Alex Lopez, the ex-husband of Diana Zubiri, died of liver cirrhosis on September 14, 2010. Liver cirrhosis is a chronic disease that causes scarring and damage to the liver, which affects its ability to function properly. Some of the common causes of liver cirrhosis are chronic alcohol abuse, viral hepatitis, autoimmune hepatitis, and genetic disorders.

Lopez was diagnosed with liver cirrhosis in 2009, after experiencing symptoms such as jaundice, abdominal swelling, fatigue, and weight loss. He underwent several treatments, including a liver transplant, but his condition did not improve. He died at the age of 32, leaving behind his wife and son.

Liver cirrhosis is a serious and life-threatening condition that can lead to complications such as bleeding, infections, liver cancer, and liver failure. It can also affect other organs, such as the brain, kidneys, and lungs. There is no cure for liver cirrhosis, but its progression can be slowed down by treating the underlying cause, avoiding alcohol, and taking medications.
