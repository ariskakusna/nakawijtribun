The Grandaddy of 'Em All: Unveiling Jacksonville's Oldest School

Alright history buffs, this one's for you. We're diving into the deep end of Jacksonville's educational history to find the OG school that's seen it all.

There are two strong contenders in this race for the title of "Jacksonville's Oldest School." Hold onto your pencils, folks, because this might get twisty-turny.

  • Andrew Jackson High School (1927): This red-brick titan stands proudly on Main Street, boasting the title of "oldest fully accredited high school" in Duval County. Fun fact: it shares a birthday with its architectural twin, Robert E. Lee High School (now known as Riverside High School). We'll get to that story in another history lesson!

  • Stanton College Preparatory School (Founded in the Reconstruction Era): Now, this one gets interesting. Stanton College Prep claims the title of "Jacksonville's first school for African-American students." While the exact founding date is a bit fuzzy (think dusty history books), it predates Andrew Jackson High School by decades. Bonus points if you can guess why the founding date might be a little tricky to pinpoint!

So, who wins? Well, that depends on how you define "oldest school." Andrew Jackson might have the official accreditation bragging rights, but Stanton holds the torch for educational opportunity for a historically underserved community.

The Verdict: It's a tie! Both schools hold a significant place in Jacksonville's history, and we celebrate their contributions to our city's educational landscape.

Fun Fact Friday!

Did you know that Stanton College Prep was originally called "Public School Number Four"? Sounds a bit…uninspired, doesn't it? We're glad they got a cooler name eventually!

FAQ: You've Got Questions, We've Got Answers (Quick!)

How to visit Andrew Jackson High School? Easy! Head over to 3816 N. Main St. and prepare to be awestruck by its historical architecture. Just don't try to sneak into detention – those days are over!

How about Stanton College Prep? Stanton College Prep is located at 100 Stanton Rd. They offer tours, so you can get a glimpse into their rich history and vibrant school community.

How can I learn more about Jacksonville's history? The Jacksonville Historical Society is a treasure trove of information! Check out their website or visit their museum downtown.

How do I impress my friends with Jacksonville trivia? Now you can! Just tell them all about the battle for "oldest school" and watch their jaws drop.
